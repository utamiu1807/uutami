Methodology of the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

The EDA process combined quantitative data analysis and visual storytelling to examine CIF import trends and assess the effects of trade policy shifts between 2023 and 2025. All computations and visualizations were performed in Google Colab using Python libraries including pandas, matplotlib, seaborn, and statsmodels.

  • Data Integration: Merged multi-year datasets (HTS-2 & HTS-4 levels) from 2023 to 2025, standardizing column formats and currency units.
  • Cleaning & Validation: Removed duplicates, corrected country codes, and handled missing CIF values using interpolation where applicable.
  • Feature Engineering: Computed year-over-year percentage change and labeled countries by Policy Category (Deal, Not-Hit, Tariffed).
  • Visualization: Generated bar charts, trend lines, and comparative plots for top importers and policy categories.
  • Statistical Testing: Conducted regression analysis to evaluate how trade programs and HS sectors affected CIF import growth.

This systematic approach ensured that insights were both data-driven and policy-relevant, linking quantitative outcomes directly to U.S. trade policy dynamics.

Executive Summary 2 ‚Äî CIF Import Comparison (2024 vs 2025)

The comparison between 2024 and 2025 CIF import values highlights how the implementation of reciprocal tariffs and selective trade deals reshaped the U.S. import landscape. While 2024 represented a relatively neutral pre-policy period, 2025 shows clear divergence driven by each country‚Äôs policy classification ‚Äî either as a Deal, Not-Hit, or Tariffed nation.

Imports from Deal Countries such as Mexico and Canada rose significantly due to trade advantages, while Tariffed Countries ‚Äî including China, Germany, and India ‚Äî saw notable declines in import volume. Not-Hit Countries like Japan and Vietnam remained stable, indicating neutral exposure to policy change.

Overall, the 2025 data confirms a policy-driven realignment of global trade flows: the U.S. shifted import concentration toward strategic allies while reducing dependence on tariff-affected economies.

Executive Summary: The 2025 trade environment rewarded alignment and penalized conflict. U.S. import growth favored allies with negotiated trade deals, while tariffed countries experienced measurable declines. This demonstrates a pivot toward strategic partners and reduced reliance on tariff-affected economies.

Key Findings ‚Äî CIF Import Structure (2025)

  • Deal Countries Expanded: Mexico and Canada show visible import growth in 2025, reflecting benefits from preferential access.
  • Tariffed Countries Declined: China, Germany, and India saw notable reductions, confirming the impact of reciprocal tariffs.
  • Not-Hit Countries Stayed Steady: Japan and Vietnam show consistent trade volumes, indicating limited exposure to tariff changes.
  • Policy Realignment: The mix of colors (blue = Deal, green = Not-Hit, red = Tariffed) reveals that trade performance now aligns with policy categories rather than geography.
  • Trade Concentration: Most imports now come from deal and not-hit countries, confirming a deliberate policy-driven reorientation of trade flows.
