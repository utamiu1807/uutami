1Ô∏è‚É£ Executive Summary

This study examines U.S. CIF Import Values from 2023 to August 2025, focusing on the effects of reciprocal tariff policies under the Trump administration. The analysis compares pre-tariff and post-tariff periods using HTS-2 and HTS-4 level data, assessing which countries and industries gained or lost market share.

Results show a clear structural shift: countries benefiting from trade deals or tariff exemptions maintained or expanded exports, while those subject to reciprocal tariffs showed notable declines. Indonesia, Mexico, and several Southeast Asian economies exhibited growth in strategic sectors such as machinery, textiles, and consumer goods.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Data & Methods

Data were collected from multiple Excel datasets hosted in the project‚Äôs GitHub repository, combining yearly and monthly CIF import files for 2023‚Äì2025 at both HTS2 and HTS4 classification levels. Cleaning steps included:

  • Standardizing column names and units (USD millions)
  • Filtering only relevant years (2023‚Äì2025)
  • Calculating percent changes and identifying top-20 countries per year
  • Classifying product descriptions into 9 industry sectors using AI-based text categorization

The analysis was conducted in Google Colab using pandas, matplotlib, seaborn, and statsmodels. Two EDA stages (EDA 1 & EDA 2) provided a comprehensive quantitative overview.
