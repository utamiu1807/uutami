4. Impact of Tariff Policies and Trade Deals

To assess the impact of Trump-era tariff policies and trade deals, a regression analysis was conducted. Policy variables included whether a country had a reciprocal tariff, a specific Trump deal, or was classified as “Not Hit”.

The regression analysis indicates:

  • Overall Model Significance: The model was not statistically significant (Prob (F-statistic) = %.3f).
  • Model Explanatory Power (R-squared): The R-squared value (%.3f) suggests the policy variables explain very little of the variation in import growth.
  • Individual Policy Variables: None of the individual policy variables (Reciprocal Tariff, Trump Deal, Not Hit) were statistically significant predictors of the percentage change in import value between 2024 and 2025.

This suggests that for the 2024-2025 period (with partial 2025 data), other factors likely played a more significant role in driving import growth than these specific policy classifications.
